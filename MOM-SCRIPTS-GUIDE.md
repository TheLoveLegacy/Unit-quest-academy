# Scripts Database — Mom's Quick Guide

This is your place to review scripts and leave feedback. It's simple and fast.

---

## Getting Started

### 1. Go to Scripts
1. **Open Mission Control** (you'll have a link)
2. **Click "📚 Scripts"** in the left menu
3. You'll see a list of all scripts

---

## Finding Scripts to Review

### Option A: Browse the List
On the left side, you'll see all scripts. Click one to view it.

### Option B: Search
Use the **"Search scripts..."** box to find by:
- Unit name (e.g., "Life Cycles")
- Script title (e.g., "From Egg to Adult")
- Subject (e.g., "Science")
- Any words in the content

### Option C: Filter by Subject or Status
Use the dropdowns to filter:
- **Subject:** Science, History, Math, Art, Geography, Reading
- **Status:** Draft, In Review, Approved, Published, Archived

---

## Reading a Script

Once you click a script:

1. **Header section** shows:
   - Unit name and script title
   - Status badge (draft, in review, approved, etc.)
   - Subject, version, age range, duration

2. **Script content** shows:
   - 📺 **Opening Hook** — the attention-grabber for viewers
   - **Key Concepts** — what kids will learn
   - **Learning Objectives** — measurable outcomes
   - **Main Script** — the full content
   - 🎥 **Video Production Notes** — filming/animation guidance
   - 🎮 **Game Connection** — how it ties to game mechanics

## Leaving Feedback

### Step 1: Click "+ Add Feedback"
You'll see a form appear below the script.

### Step 2: Choose Feedback Type
- 💬 **Comment** — general note or observation
- 💡 **Suggestion** — idea for improvement
- ✅ **Approval** — script is ready to film
- 🔄 **Revision Needed** — requires changes before proceeding

### Step 3: Write Your Feedback
Type your comment in the text box. Be specific:

**Good:** "The opening hook about the caterpillar is great, but I'd love to see more detail about how long metamorphosis actually takes. Kids might be curious about exact timelines."

**Not helpful:** "Good work!"

### Step 4: Click "Submit Feedback"
Your feedback is instant. Adam will see it right away.

---

## Feedback Examples

### 💬 Comment
"I like the order of these concepts. They build logically."

### 💡 Suggestion
"Could we add an example of metamorphosis in real life that kids have seen? Like ant colonies going through changes?"

### ✅ Approval
"This is ready to film! The script flows well and will engage 7-10 year olds."

### 🔄 Revision Needed
"The learning objectives are clear, but Activity 2 needs more details. How long does it take? What materials do kids need?"

---

## Tracking Your Feedback

### Open Feedback
Scripts show feedback you've submitted that Adam hasn't resolved yet.

### Resolved Feedback
Once Adam addresses your feedback, it moves to the "Resolved" section with his notes about what changed.

---

## Key Tips

✅ **Be specific** — Instead of "confusing," say "The part about photosynthesis could use a simple diagram."

✅ **Reference sections** — Use section names (Intro, Concept 1, Activities) so Adam knows exactly what you mean.

✅ **Ask questions** — If something isn't clear, ask! Adam will clarify.

✅ **Approve when ready** — Use the ✅ Approval type when you think a script is ready to film.

✅ **Don't worry about grammar** — Focus on content and clarity, not typos.
